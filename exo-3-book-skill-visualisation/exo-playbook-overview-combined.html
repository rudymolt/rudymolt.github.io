Goal-setting architecture

ExO 3.0 combines DRIVE and SHAPE around a machine-readable MTP.

The skill treats the Massive Transformative Purpose as a protocol, not a slogan. DRIVE creates speed and intelligence density; SHAPE provides the human, legal, trust, and control structure that prevents speed from becoming fragility.

Operating core

The Intelligence Stack is the firm's machine-speed learning loop.

The stack turns purpose into action and feedback. GOVERN/ASSURE is not a final review layer; it runs across every layer with logging, evals, rollback, and human review.

Migration playbook

REWRITE is a six-phase sequence with explicit exit artifacts.

The order matters. The playbook starts with the future architecture, then scores readiness, extracts knowledge and data boundaries, decomposes work, proves workflows in parallel, and only then rewires the organization.

1
BackcastBACKCAST & DEFINE
Start from the future goal, define the first Edge Twin or Direct Mode target, and instantiate the five design conditions.
Output: Goal-setting architecture and Wave 1 mandate.
2
ReadinessASSESS & PREPARE
Score organizational drag, AI elevation, work architecture, autonomy, network structure, reinvention cadence, and tacit knowledge access.
Output: Readiness Score, on-ramp, governance maturity.
3
KnowledgeEXTRACT
Capture tacit knowledge, workflow context, and data requirements before handing work to agents.
Output: Workflow Data Manifest and HIDO data-checklist answers: six questions for each data object.
4
Work auditDIAGNOSE & STRIP
Decompose roles into tasks, score agent readiness, remove work that exists only because the old firm was slow.
Output: Task Decomposition Matrix.
5
ProofBUILD & PROVE
Run decision-handover waves in shadow mode, compare human and agent decisions, capture overrides, and prove falling override rates.
Output: Parallel-run proof and rollback path.
6
Operating modelREWIRE & EVOLVE
Replace static org-chart logic with small accountable teams and make stop/fix/rollback review a regular leadership routine.
Output: New operating cadence and governance loop.
Deployment choice

Small firms transform directly; larger firms need an Edge Twin.

The skill uses team size and resistance to change as the practical fork. A small company can become the edge. A larger company needs a CEO-sponsored, board-mandated edge venture that learns safely outside the mothership.

Direct Mode

Use when: roughly 50 people or fewer, with low resistance to change.

Apply REWRITE to the whole company. Stand up MVIS in week one and run a 90-day sprint on the highest-coordination workflow.

Team size and resistance to change

Edge Mode

Use when: more than 50 people, regulated complexity, or strong department boundaries.

Spawn a 3-5 person Edge Twin with agent cluster, CEO sponsorship, board mandate, and its own governed workflow boundary.

Proof metricHuman override rate falls while quality and safety hold.
Control plane

Four pillars decide whether agents are safe enough to deploy.

The skill caps ambition when governance is weak. New agent classes should not deploy until each pillar scores at least 3 out of 5.

Trusted Evals

Quantified tests and thresholds that catch drift before customers do.

Searchable Logs

Correlation IDs, traceable decisions, tool calls, data access, and rationale.

Granular Rollback

Recover by workflow, agent, decision class, record, or action without freezing the firm.

Human Review Queue

Named owners, escalation rules, and clear approval thresholds for risky action.
